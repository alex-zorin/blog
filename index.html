Alexander Zorin’s blog

Hello, earthlings!

I’m Alexander Zorin and this is my blog. Well, there are no articles here yet, but it’s a temporary issue. The blog has just been created, at the beginning of 2023, and I am going to run it as soon as possible. The vast majority of notes will be on technical topics.

I write articles for human beings and search engines, scripts and algorithms for computers, administer Unix systems (almost since the beginning of the millennium). The main motivation for producing a blog was that I created several projects which, as I hope, could be interesting for many brothers in mind. Here is a little list of the subjects I intend to cover first:

  • an improved and optimized keyboard layout convenient for both programming and writing prose, which has the same arrangement of punctuation and special characters in both standard Latin and national layout;
  • a universal textual tester I use for debug and verification purposes;
  • a special mark-up language, similar to Markdown or reST, but enhancing their ideas in many ways;
  • some tools for shell programming (which tends to be called “Bash”, independent of the actual language, even it obeys the POSIX specification);
  • and much more.

I’d like to discuss some philosophical or social topics too, but I’m afraid it’s impossible for security reasons. I’m on the side of unhappy ordinary people; you can read the rest “between the lines”.

From time to time, I’m mistaken for one of the creators of Zorin OS Linux distribution. No. I have no relation to that team. If you’re looking for information about Zorin OS, you should visit zorin.com or/and blog.zorin.com.

But before you go, memorize two words to get here in the future: “Zorin” and “blog” — and one day you will find here a lot of valuable items about Linux, shell programming and many related topics.

OK.
